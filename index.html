Nikola Tesla

Inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist.

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla life:

  • 1856 - Born in the village of Smiljan nearby Gospić, Croatia.
  • 1861 - Attend primary school in Smiljan.
  • 1870 - Attend high school at the Higher Real Gymnasium.
  • 1873 - Graduate, finished a four-year term in three years.
  • 1875 - Enrolled at the Imperial-Royal Technical College in Graz.
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest, Hungary, to work under Tivadar Puskás at a telegraph company, the Budapest Telephone Exchange.
  • 1882 - Start working in Paris for the Continental Edison Company.
  • 1884 - Moving to the United States.
  • 1885 - Left Edison company and founded Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing.
  • 1886 - Tesla developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current (AC).
  • 1891 - Patented Tesla coil.
  • 1890-1906 - Tesla spent a great deal of his time and fortune on a series of projects trying to develop the transmission of electrical power without wires.
  • 1917 - He was awarded the Edison Medal, the highest decoration of the American Institute of Electrical Engineers.
  • 1943 - dies at the age of 87 in New York, USA.
